Triangle — Where Does the Amber Go?

Four options. Each tells a different story about what matters.

Option 0 — Baseline

All Teal — No Amber

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

Unity and restraint. One color = one voice. No hierarchy within the symbol — every line carries equal weight. The meaning comes from the shape itself, not from color coding.

When to use: When you want the brand to feel cohesive, serious, and understated. Works well at small sizes where color distinctions get lost anyway.

Trade-off: Less visual interest. No focal point. The eye doesn't know where to land.

Option 1 — Current Design

Angular Path in Amber (the "checkmark")

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

The checkmark = validation, completion, "yes." This angular path resembles a checkmark — the universal symbol for approval, achievement, task done. By highlighting it in amber, you're saying: "We deliver. We confirm. We make it happen."

The warmth is grounded. Amber sits at the base of the triangle, suggesting warmth that's rooted, stable, foundational — not floating or abstract.

When to use: When the brand message is about results, execution, getting things across the finish line. "Know what you want. Don't settle for less" aligns with this — it's about achieving, not just dreaming.

Option 2

Diagonal Line in Amber (the "shortcut")

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

The diagonal = disruption, seeing differently, the unconventional path. This line cuts across the expected geometry. It doesn't follow the triangle's edges — it creates its own route from apex toward the foundation.

The warmth comes from the top. Amber originates at the peak and descends — like insight flowing down, or vision informing action. It's aspirational warmth.

When to use: When the brand message is about thinking differently, challenging conventions, finding angles others miss. This suits "coming from a small village, often not believed, often years ahead" — the outsider perspective.

Option 3

All Internal Lines in Amber

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

Interior = the soul. Exterior = the structure. Teal forms the outer boundary — the framework, the rules, the visible shape. Amber fills the interior — the human element, the intuition, what actually moves within the system.

Like the jellyfish. Translucent bell (structure) with a warm glowing center (life). This directly mirrors the Turritopsis imagery: the geometry holds the spark.

When to use: When the brand message is about the balance between structure and soul, logic and intuition, AI and humanity. "Artificial Intelligence & Longevity" — the machine (teal) and the living (amber).

Summary: What Each Choice Says

Option 0 — All Teal "We are unified, serious, no frills"
Option 1 — Checkmark "We deliver, we execute, we get it done"
Option 2 — Diagonal "We see differently, we take unconventional paths"
Option 3 — All Interior "We balance structure with soul, tech with humanity"
