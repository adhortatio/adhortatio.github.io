Golden Spiral — Where Does the Amber Go?

Five options. The spiral has three elements: rectangles (structure), curve (growth), center (origin).

Option 0 — Baseline

All Teal — No Amber

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

Pure mathematics. No warmth. The golden ratio speaks for itself — φ needs no embellishment. This is intellectual, precise, austere. The beauty is in the math alone.

When to use: When you want to emphasize rigor, precision, and mathematical elegance above all else. Appeals to those who see the spiral and already understand its significance.

Trade-off: Can feel cold. No focal point. Nothing says "life" — it's pure abstraction.

Option 1 — Current Design

Center Dot in Amber (the "seed")

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

The origin is alive. Everything spirals outward from this single warm point — the seed, the first cell, the initial spark. The mathematics (teal) grows from something living (amber).

Longevity begins here. The Turritopsis regenerates from its center. A single point contains infinite potential. This is where the spiral starts and where it could, theoretically, return.

When to use: When the message is about origins, beginnings, the small thing that becomes large. "Coming from a small village" — the dot is the village, the spiral is what it becomes.

Option 2

Spiral Curve in Amber (the "growth")

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

Growth itself is warm. The rectangles are cold scaffolding — the underlying math, the invisible structure. But the curve — the actual path of expansion — glows with life. Growth is not mechanical; it's organic.

The visible vs the hidden. Teal rectangles = the theory. Amber spiral = the practice. You build on mathematics, but what people see and experience is the living curve.

When to use: When the message emphasizes growth, expansion, scaling. The journey matters more than the origin. "Charting new horizons" — the charting (the curve) is the action.

Option 3

Rectangles in Amber (the "hidden structure")

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

The hidden foundation is warm. The golden rectangles are the secret — the underlying ratio that most people never see. By making them amber, you're saying: "The invisible structure matters. The math beneath the surface is where the magic lives."

AI & Longevity connection. The algorithms (amber scaffolding) that power AI are hidden, but they're the warm engine. The visible output (teal curve) is cool and precise, but it's built on something deeper.

When to use: When you want to honor the unseen work, the infrastructure, the research. "We show you the curve, but we built the rectangles."

Option 4

Spiral + Center in Amber, Rectangles in Teal

adhortatio
adhortatio

Branding Rationale

Life flows, structure supports. The amber is everything organic — the seed and its growth, the origin and its expansion. The teal rectangles are the framework that makes it possible but stays in the background.

Mirrors the triangle Option 3. Just as that option made all internal lines amber (soul inside structure), this makes all "living" elements amber. Consistent brand logic across both symbols.

When to use: When you want maximum warmth while keeping the mathematical framework visible. "We're alive, and we show our work."
